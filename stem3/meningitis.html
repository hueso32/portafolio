All about Meningitis

What is Meningitis?

Meningitis is an inflamation of the meninges caused by viral or bacterial and marked by intense headache and fever, sensitivity to light, and muscular rigidity, leading to convulsions, delirium and death.

Symptons

  • Sudden high fever
  • stiff neck
  • severe headache that seems different than normal
  • headache with nausea or vomiting
  • confusion or difficulty concentrating
  • seizures
  • sleepiness or difficulty waking
  • sensitivity to light
  • no apetite or thirst
  • skin rash

    • why is it dangerous?

    Meningitis is dangerous because Within a matter of hours, this disease can cause inflammation of the brain and spinal cord and widespread infection throughout the body, which can cause death.
